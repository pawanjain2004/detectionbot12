The problem we tackle

Many farmers rely on manual inspection to detect plant diseases — time-consuming and often inaccurate. Early symptoms are easily missed, leading to widespread infection, excessive pesticide use, reduced crop yield, and financial losses. In India, up to 40% of crops are affected by diseases, with the country losing approximately ₹50,000+ crore annually. According to the UN FAO (2022), 224.3 million people in India are undernourished. Agriaid helps farmers identify diseases early so they can treat crops before significant damage — without the cost of expensive machinery.

How Agriaid works

The rover navigates agricultural areas and captures images of plant leaves at regular intervals. Our pipeline uses YOLOv8 to detect and crop individual leaves, then a classification model predicts whether each leaf is healthy or unhealthy and shows the result with confidence on the web app. The rover is controlled and viewed via a live camera feed from the dashboard — each captured image is stored and results are displayed alongside it.

What inspired us

A family trip to Baran, Rajasthan, exposed the real challenges farmers face — crops with abnormal spots and discoloration, and the difficulty of early detection with limited resources. That experience led to building Agriaid to make a change at scale.

What's next

Reaching out to NGOs and more farmers to test and refine the rover. After the testing phase, we aim to automate the rover further and scale so more farms can use Agriaid to increase crop yield and reduce wastage.
